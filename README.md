# In-Silico-Drug-Designing.
In Silico Drug Designing for Multidrug-Resistant Mycobacterium tuberculosis (MDR-TB) Targeting Enoyl-Acyl Carrier Protein Reductase (InhA).

## Project Overview

This repository contains my Bachelor's Final Year Project (FYP), which focuses on **structure-based in silico drug discovery** against multidrug-resistant *Mycobacterium tuberculosis* (MDR-TB). The study targets the **Enoyl-Acyl Carrier Protein Reductase (InhA)** enzyme, a validated drug target involved in mycolic acid biosynthesis.

The objective of this research was to identify novel lead compounds using computational approaches, including virtual screening, molecular docking, ADMET analysis, and molecular dynamics simulations.

---

## Objectives

* Retrieve and prepare the InhA protein structure (PDB ID: 4TRO)
* Predict the active binding site
* Screen natural compounds for drug-likeness
* Evaluate ADMET and hERG toxicity
* Perform molecular docking against InhA
* Validate binding stability using 100 ns Molecular Dynamics simulations
* Identify promising lead compounds for future anti-tuberculosis drug development

---

## Workflow

1. Protein Retrieval (PDB ID: 4TRO)
2. Protein Preparation and Energy Minimization
3. Structure Validation (Ramachandran Plot)
4. Active Site Prediction (CASTp)
5. Ligand Library Preparation (CMNPD)
6. Drug-Likeness and ADMET Screening (SwissADME)
7. hERG Toxicity Prediction
8. Molecular Docking (PyRx / AutoDock Vina)
9. Interaction Analysis
10. Molecular Dynamics Simulation (100 ns)
11. Lead Compound Identification

## Key Findings

* Successfully prepared and validated the InhA protein structure.
* Screened 500 marine-derived natural compounds.
* Selected seven compounds with favorable drug-likeness and safety profiles.
* Identified **Acetyllaurefucin** as the top-performing compound.
* Acetyllaurefucin demonstrated stronger predicted binding affinity than the reference ligand (Triclosan).
* Molecular Dynamics simulations confirmed stable protein-ligand interactions over a 100 ns simulation.

---



## Disclaimer

This project is entirely based on **computational (in silico) analyses**. The identified lead compound requires **experimental validation**, including in vitro and in vivo studies, before any therapeutic conclusions can be drawn.
